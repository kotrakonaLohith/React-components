## Usage

### Types

#### Unordered lists
Use to present content of equal status or value.

<ul className="list mb-32">
  <li>List item 1</li>
  <li>List item 2</li>
  <li>List item 3</li>
</ul>

#### Ordered lists
Use to present content with an implied sequence and order.

<ol className="list mb-32">
  <li>List item 1</li>
  <li>List item 2</li>
  <li>List item 3</li>
</ol>

___

## Best Practices

### General

<DosDonts>
  <Do>use lists to present simple pieces of information</Do>
  <Dont>use lists to present complex information or lengthy content</Dont>
  <Dont>overuse lists, as they can lose their effectiveness</Dont>
  <Dont>use lists to present sets of unrelated data</Dont>
  <Dont>create groupings of list items</Dont>
  <Dont>use ordered lists when the sequence or count of items is not important</Dont>
</DosDonts>

### Content

<DosDonts>
  <Do>arrange list items in a logical way</Do>
  <Do>use the same format for all items in a list</Do>
  <Do>use list items that have approximately similiar lengths</Do>
  <Dont>repeat the same word at the beginning of each list item</Dont>
  <Dont>include more than X items in a single list</Dont>
</DosDonts>
