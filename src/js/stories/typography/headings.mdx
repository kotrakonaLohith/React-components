# Headings

## Regular Headings

Headings styles are applied with the class `heading` and a modifier (eg. `-primary`, `-secondary`, etc) or utlity classes (eg. `text-base`, `text-sm`, etc.) Spacing should always be applied with utility classes (eg. `mb-32` in this example.)

<div>
  <h1 className="heading-primary mb-32">H1 - Open Sans Light, 66</h1>
  <h2 className="heading-secondary mb-32">H2 - Open Sans Regular, 36</h2>
  <h3 className="heading-tertiary mb-32">H3 - Open Sans Regular, 20</h3>
  <h4 className="heading text-base mb-32">H4 - Open Sans Bold, 16</h4>
  <h5 className="heading text-xs mb-32">H5 - Open Sans Bold, 14</h5>
  <h4 className="heading-alt text-sm mb-32">H4 Alt - Open Sans Bold - All Caps - Spaced, 15</h4>
  <h5 className="heading-alt text-2xs mb-32">H5 Alt - Open Sans Bold - All Caps - Spaced, 13</h5>
</div>

```tsx
<h1 className="heading-primary">H1 - Open Sans Light, 66</h1>
<h2 className="heading-secondary">H2 - Open Sans Regular, 36</h2>
<h3 className="heading-tertiary">H3 - Open Sans Regular, 20</h3>
<h4 className="heading text-base">H4 - Open Sans Bold, 16</h4>

<h5 className="heading-alt text-2xs">H5 Alt - Open Sans Bold - All Caps - Spaced, 13</h5>
```

## Headings with topper

Headings with toppers combine the headings above with either a step counter topper or a back link topper.

<div className="mb-32">
  <span className="heading-topper">Step 1 of 2</span>
  <h1 className="heading-primary">Delhi South Metropolitan</h1>
</div>
<div className="mb-32">
  <a href="#" className="heading-topper">
    <LeftIcon aria-hidden="true" /> Members
  </a>
  <h1 className="heading-primary">Delhi South Metropolitan</h1>
</div>

```tsx
import LeftIcon from 'path/to/left.svg'

<div className="mb-32">
  <span className="heading-topper">Step 1 of 2</span>
  <h1 className="heading-primary">Delhi South Metropolitan</h1>
</div>
<div className="mb-32">
  <a href="#" className="heading-topper">
    <LeftIcon aria-hidden="true" /> Members
  </a>
  <h1 className="heading-primary">Delhi South Metropolitan</h1>
</div>
```
